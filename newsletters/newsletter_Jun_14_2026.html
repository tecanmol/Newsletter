Research Corner

The Hidden Power of Scaling Factor in LoRA Optimization

This paper dives into LoRA, a popular technique for fine-tuning large language models efficiently. It explains that the 'scaling factor' you often set isn't just a minor tweak; it's actually super important for how well your model learns and optimizes.

Person Identification from Contextual Motion

Researchers are exploring how to identify people not by their face, but by their unique movement patterns. This paper describes a model that can figure out who someone is just by how they walk or move in different situations, like in surveillance or authentication.

Once-for-All: Scalable Simultaneous Forecasting via Equilibrium State Estimation

This paper introduces a new way to make predictions for multiple interconnected systems all at once, like forecasting economic trends or healthcare needs. Instead of separate models, it tries to find an "equilibrium state" for everything, making forecasting more coordinated and efficient.
