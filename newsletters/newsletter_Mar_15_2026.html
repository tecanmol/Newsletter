🤖 This Week in AI

Mar 15, 2026
AI's capabilities stretched further this week, from massive context windows to physical deployments in factories and discussions around military use. It was a week where theoretical advancements met tangible real-world applications, pushing the boundaries of what AI can do in both software and hardware. We also saw major players focusing on partnerships and governance as the field matures.

1M context is now generally available for Opus 4.6 and Sonnet 4.6

Claude's latest models, Opus 4.6 and Sonnet 4.6, now offer a massive 1 million token context window for all users. This means they can process entire books or hundreds of pages of documents in a single prompt.

💡 Why it matters: This drastically expands the practical applications for LLMs, allowing them to handle much larger, complex tasks without losing context.

BMW puts humanoid robots to work in Germany—and Europe’s factories are watching

BMW has started a pilot project deploying AEON humanoid robots from Hexagon Robotics in its Leipzig plant in Germany. This marks the first automotive deployment of these wheeled humanoids.

💡 Why it matters: It signals a significant move towards physical AI in manufacturing, potentially setting a precedent for wider adoption in European factories.

A defense official reveals how AI chatbots could be used for targeting decisions

A US Defense Department official disclosed that the military might use generative AI systems to rank targets and recommend strike priorities. These recommendations would still be vetted by humans.

💡 Why it matters: It shows a clear, if cautious, path for AI's integration into critical military decision-making processes, raising ethical and operational questions.

Launching the Claude Partner Network

Anthropic launched its Claude Partner Network, aiming to integrate Claude's AI capabilities into various enterprise applications and workflows. The network will help businesses leverage Claude more effectively.

💡 Why it matters: It indicates a focus on enterprise adoption and scaling through strategic partnerships, making Claude more accessible for business solutions.

Why physical AI is becoming manufacturing’s next advantage

Manufacturers are increasingly turning to physical AI, which involves robots and AI working together in the real world, to overcome labor constraints and increasing complexity. This goes beyond traditional automation.

💡 Why it matters: It highlights a broader industry trend where advanced AI is moving from digital to physical operations, reshaping the future of manufacturing.

PACED: Distillation at the Frontier of Student Competence

This paper proposes a smarter way to train smaller language models (students) from larger ones (teachers) by focusing only on problems that are challenging but not impossible for the student. It avoids wasting effort on already mastered or far-too-difficult tasks.

Exoskeleton Control through Learning to Reduce Biological Joint Moments in Simulations

Researchers are using AI simulations to train controllers for exoskeletons that can help reduce the strain on human joints. This approach uses physics-based reinforcement learning to teach the exoskeleton how to assist effectively.

Toward Complex-Valued Neural Networks for Waveform Generation

This research explores using neural networks that handle complex numbers to improve how AI generates audio waveforms. It's about making AI-generated sound more natural and expressive, especially for vocoders.
