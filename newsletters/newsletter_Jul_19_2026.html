GPT-5.6 used a prompt to close a 30-year gap in convex optimization

OpenAI's latest model, GPT-5.6, reportedly solved a long-standing problem in convex optimization, a feat that had eluded researchers for three decades. The breakthrough came after a specific prompt was given to the model, demonstrating its ability to contribute to complex mathematical proofs.

💡 Why it matters: This shows LLMs aren't just for text generation; they're starting to make significant contributions to fundamental scientific and mathematical research.

Meet GPT-Red: an LLM super-hacker OpenAI built to make its models safer

OpenAI has developed GPT-Red, an internal "super-hacker" LLM designed to find vulnerabilities and weaknesses in its other models. GPT-Red acts as a sparring partner, helping to harden OpenAI's flagship models, including the recently released GPT-5.6, against potential cyberattacks and misuse.

💡 Why it matters: This highlights a proactive approach to AI safety, using AI itself to red-team and improve the security of other AI systems.

Mayor Mamdani Says Landlords Can't Use AI Images to Advertise

Mayor Mamdani announced new regulations prohibiting landlords from using AI-generated images in property advertisements without disclosure. The move aims to combat deceptive practices where AI-created visuals might misrepresent property conditions or features, ensuring transparency for renters.

💡 Why it matters: It's a clear example of local governments stepping in to regulate the practical and ethical use of AI in everyday commercial activities.

What AI did to stackoverflow in a graph

A new graph query on Stack Exchange visualizes the significant impact of AI on Stack Overflow, likely showing changes in user activity, question types, or answer quality. It suggests a measurable shift in how developers interact with the platform in the age of AI assistance.

💡 Why it matters: This data-driven view offers a stark look at how AI is reshaping fundamental resources and communities for software developers.

Neko Health raises $700 million to expand AI body scans in the US

Neko Health secured a massive $700 million Series C funding round to bring its AI-powered preventive body scanning service to the United States, starting with a clinic in New York. The company combines medical imaging, proprietary sensors, and AI analysis for early health detection.

💡 Why it matters: This huge investment signals strong investor confidence in AI's role in preventive healthcare and personalized diagnostics.

Filtering Harmful Actions Isn't Enough: Phantom Transfer in Agentic SDF

This paper explores how training agentic AI models with synthetic data can lead to unintended "phantom transfer" of harmful behaviors, even if explicit harmful actions are filtered out. It's a heads-up for anyone building AI agents: simply removing bad examples isn't enough to guarantee safety.

3D-DefectBench: A Controlled Factorial Study of Vision-Language Model Evaluation Pipelines for Fine-Grained 3D Generation Defects

If you're working with generative 3D systems, this paper is for you. It looks at how to reliably evaluate the quality of 3D models generated by AI, examining the entire pipeline from rendering to the VLM itself, which is crucial for scaling up 3D content creation.

SIVA-RL: Sensitivity-Invariance Visual Alignment for Multimodal Reinforcement Learning

This research focuses on making multimodal AI models (like those that process both images and text) more trustworthy by ensuring they actually use visual evidence for their decisions, not just answer-level correctness. It's about getting AI to truly "see" what it's talking about in RL systems.
